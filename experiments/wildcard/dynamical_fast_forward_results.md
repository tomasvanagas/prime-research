# Dynamical Fast-Forward: Results

**Script:** dynamical_fast_forward.py

## What Was Tested
Whether primes can be characterized by a fast-forwardable dynamical system: gap recurrence prediction, Beatty sequence decomposition, symbolic dynamics/sofic shifts, Collatz-like iterations, and matrix group orbits (SL(2,Z) / Selberg).

## Key Findings
- Linear gap prediction (AR models up to order 20): R^2 near 0, gaps are essentially unpredictable from previous gaps
- Primes are not a Beatty sequence and cannot be decomposed as a finite union of Beatty sequences (density mismatch)
- Binary prime indicator has maximal block complexity -- not generated by any sofic shift or substitution system
- No simple recurrence p(n+1) = f(p(n), n) found; Wilson-based iterations require O(p) factorial computation
- Selberg connection: computing nth eigenvalue of the Laplacian on Gamma\H is equivalent to computing zeta zeros

## Verdict
**CLOSED**
**Failure Mode:** Equivalence (E) -- all dynamical formulations either have maximal complexity (no fast-forwarding) or reduce to computing zeta zeros.

## One-Line Summary
Five dynamical fast-forward approaches (gap AR, Beatty, sofic, iteration, Selberg) all fail -- gaps unpredictable, maximal complexity.
