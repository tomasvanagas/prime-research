# Partial Sums Recurrence Test Results

**Date:** 2026-04-05  
**Script:** `experiments/analytic/zeta_structure/partial_sums_recurrence.py`

## Setup

Partial sums of simplified explicit formula:

    S_K(x) = sum_{k=1}^{K} cos(gamma_k * log(x)) / sqrt(1/4 + gamma_k^2)

Using 1000 zeta zeros, tested at x = 100, 1000, 10000, 100000.

## Results

### 1. Linear Recurrence (order 1-20)

**NEGATIVE.** No linear recurrence of order up to 20 fits the sequence {S_K}.

Best relative residuals (all >> 0.01 threshold):

| x       | Best order | Rel residual |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| 100     | 20        | 2.33e-02    |
| 1,000   | 19        | 5.96e-02    |
| 10,000  | 20        | 1.57e-02    |
| 100,000 | 20        | 2.45e-02    |

Residuals decrease monotonically with order (overfitting), not a sharp drop at some order r (which would indicate a true recurrence). The sequence is not generated by a constant-coefficient linear recurrence of any tested order.

### 2. Nonlinear Recurrence (polynomial degree 2, 3)

**NEGATIVE.** Testing S_{K+1} = f(S_K, S_{K-1}) for polynomial f:

| x       | Degree 2 rel | Degree 3 rel |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| 100     | 3.52e-02    | 3.33e-02    |
| 1,000   | 7.31e-02    | 6.99e-02    |
| 10,000  | 2.50e-02    | 2.21e-02    |
| 100,000 | 3.79e-02    | 3.62e-02    |

Nonlinear models offer negligible improvement over the best linear recurrence. The partial sums do not satisfy low-degree polynomial recurrences.

### 3. Difference Recurrence

**STRONGLY NEGATIVE.** The differences d_K = S_K - S_{K-1} = cos(gamma_K * log(x)) / sqrt(1/4 + gamma_K^2) show relative residuals of 87-100% for all orders tested. This is expected: individual terms are essentially pseudorandom oscillations modulated by a slowly decaying envelope.

### 4. Convergence Rate

Neither geometric nor algebraic decay fits well (all R^2 < 0.5):

| x       | Better fit  | Geo rate | Geo R^2 | Alg alpha | Alg R^2 |
|---------|------------|---------|---------|----------|---------|
| 100     | Algebraic  | 0.998   | 0.209   | 1.00     | 0.223   |
| 1,000   | Geometric  | 0.997   | 0.450   | 1.00     | 0.313   |
| 10,000  | Algebraic  | 0.997   | 0.279   | 1.27     | 0.300   |
| 100,000 | Geometric  | 0.997   | 0.408   | 1.41     | 0.343   |

The convergence is **erratic, not smooth**. The error |S_K - S_1000| does not decay monotonically -- it oscillates wildly. This is consistent with the GUE-random phases of zeta zeros making each new term an essentially unpredictable perturbation.

### 5. Individual Term Structure

Term magnitudes decay algebraically: |term_k| ~ k^{-0.75} (approximately).

This matches the expected rate: gamma_k ~ 2*pi*k/log(k) by density of zeros, so 1/sqrt(gamma_k^2) ~ 1/gamma_k ~ log(k)/k, and the envelope |term_k| ~ 1/gamma_k ~ k^{-0.7 to -0.8} in the range tested.

Autocorrelation at lag-1 is low and inconsistent in sign across x values, confirming that successive terms behave pseudo-randomly.

## Interpretation

The partial sums {S_K(x)} do **not** satisfy any detectable recurrence -- linear, nonlinear, or on differences. This is a direct consequence of:

1. **Quasi-random phases:** cos(gamma_k * log(x)) where gamma_k are GUE-distributed zeros. The phases gamma_k * log(x) mod 2*pi are equidistributed (for most x) with correlations governed by Montgomery's pair correlation, making the partial sums behave like random walks with decreasing step sizes.

2. **Slowly decaying envelope:** |term_k| ~ k^{-0.75}, so individual contributions remain significant even for large K. The sum does not converge fast enough for early terms to dominate.

3. **No hidden periodicity:** A recurrence would imply the sequence lies in a finite-dimensional space, but the effective dimension grows with K because each new zero gamma_k brings genuinely new information (phases are linearly independent over rationals with probability 1).

## Conclusion

**CLOSED PATH.** Partial sums of the explicit formula over zeta zeros do not satisfy finite-order recurrences. This rules out any shortcut that would compute S_K from a bounded number of earlier partial sums, confirming that all K zeros must be individually summed.

This is consistent with the information-theoretic barrier: the oscillatory part of pi(x) encodes O(x^{1/2}/log(x)) bits from the zeros, and no compression of this sum via recurrence is possible.
