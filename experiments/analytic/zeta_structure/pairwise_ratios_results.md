## Interpretation

The KS test detects a statistically significant but **practically negligible** difference
(KS stat = 0.0039, p = 1.28e-06). The direction is that zeta zero ratios are marginally
**farther** from simple rationals than the null model -- the opposite of what would be
needed for exploitation. The median errors differ by only ~0.4% (8.466e-05 vs 8.429e-05).
The mean error for zeta is slightly higher (1.73e-04 vs 1.66e-04), consistent with the
known GUE repulsion making zeta spacings slightly more regular than shuffled spacings,
which shifts some ratios away from simple fractions.

The Mann-Whitney test on the extreme tail (closest 1% of matches) shows **no significant
difference** (p = 0.38), confirming no anomalous clustering near rationals.

## Conclusion

No exploitable rational structure found in pairwise zeta zero ratios. The zeros are, if
anything, marginally *farther* from simple rationals than random (consistent with GUE
repulsion), and the effect is far too small to be useful. This closes the "rational ratio
shortcut" avenue for prime computation.
